Calculus II, Fall 2017
Standards

As detailed on the syllabus, your grade in this course will be determined by your proficiency on a variety of standards. (This is known as Standards Based Grading.)

Here is the list of standards that form the basis for this class, along with guiding questions that address each standard. You will be assessed on these standards throughout the semester.

Mathematical Maturity

Standard M1. Definitions. Do you understand what a definition is? Are you able to write an explicit definition statement? Are you able to explain what the definition means in your own words? Are you able to give examples and non-examples of the definition?

Standard M2. Key Theorems. Do you understand what a theorem is? Do you understand what a hypothesis is? Do you understand what a conclusion is? What is the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus? What is the Mean Value Theorem? What are the consequences of each?

Standard M3. Calculator and Computer Skills. Can you use (your calculator / Wolfram Alpha / Mathematica) to take definite integrals? Can you use Mathematica to plot functions? Can you use Mathematica's Documentation Center? Can you use Mathematica to compute the volume of the goblet in your project?

Standard M4. Working in Groups. Are you able to discuss mathematical concepts with your classmates? Can you work on in-class activites with your peers? Can you contribute to a class discussion constructively and supportively? Can you work together on your project as a group? Can you work toward a common goal with others?

Understanding Concepts

Standard 2. Antiderivatives. What is an antiderivative? Do you know basic antiderivatives by heart?

Standard 5. Riemann Sums. Can you use rectangles to approximate area under a curve? Can you set up a Riemann sum, both theoretically and for a given function? What is the midpoint rule? What is the average value of a function?

Standard 6. The Concept of an Integral. What is a definite integral? When does it exist? What does it mean for a function to be integrable? What is the difference between definite and indefinite integrals?

Standard 13. Inverse Functions. What is the inverse of a function? What are the properties of inverse functions? What is the general form of a derivative of an inverse function? Can you take the derivative of an inverse trigonometric function?

Standard 14. Understanding Logarithmic and Exponential Functions. What is a logarithmic function? What is ln? What is a logarithm base a? What is an exponential function? What is ex? What is an exponential base a?

Computational Prowess

Standard 4. Sigma Notation. Can you convert from sums to sigma noation? Can you convert from sigma notation to a sum? Can you evaluate sums involving Σi, Σi2, Σi3?

Standard 7. Properties of Integrals. What algebraic properties do definite and indefinite integrals obey? Sums

Standard 9. Basic Integrals. What is the Net Change Theorem? Can you evaluate basic integrals involving polynomials? Involving trigonometric functions? Involving exponential and logarithmic functions?

Standard 11. FTC Integrals. Can you evaluate expressions involving both derivatives and integrals?

Standard 12. Substitution Rule. Can you correctly apply the substitution rule?

Standard 15. Calculating with Logarithmic and Exponential Functions. What are the properties of logarithms? What are the properties of logarithms?

Standard 17. Areas. Can you calculate the area between curves? Can you set up and integrate with respect to x? Can you set up and integrate with respect to y?

Standard 18. Volumes. Do you understand the theory of calculating volume by adding slices? Can you set up integrals to calculate volume by adding slices?

Standard 19. Cylindrical Shells. Can you calculate volume using cylindrical shells?

Standard 21. Arc Length. Can you find the arc length of a curve? Do you understand the theory of the integral used to calculate arc length?

Standard 22. Solving Differential Equations. Can you solve a differential equation using separable equations? Can you visualize solutions to differential equations using vector fields?

Applications of Calculus

Standard 3. Particle Motion. Do you understand how to find position from velocity or acceleration? Are you able to understand the relationship between position and velocity as area under a curve?

Standard 16. Differential Equations. What is a differential equation? What is an initial condition? Can you set up a differential equation for a given situation?
